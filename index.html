MEMORIES AFTER 100 YEARS

I had a little brother ‘H’. This was told to me and my sister when we were children. “Boil some chicken meat and give the child to drink, some soup. If not, the child will die”, said my grandmother to my mother. My mother answered: “No” because she said”. Dr had told her not to give anything to eat or drink because the child is suffering from stomach problem. Sadly, the child died, about 2 years old. From then on my mother took her children never again to doctors or believed in whatever they said. My parents were good and polite, educated us in good behaviour from childhood even though they didn’t practice religion but believed to be good catholic family from my mother’s and anglicans from father’s since it was in 1800. They loved social life - trips, parties more than church going - Holy Mass or pious practices. My mother especially asked us to pray before going to bed and made a sign of the cross on our forehead with the words. “God Bless you”

I enjoyed our stay with grandpa and grandma. Grandpa was very pious and I saw him praying and so, I liked to stay with him in his office while he worked. He had a title of Sir belonging to the British Administrative System. I did not know his name except grandpa. I stayed longer with my grandparents than my sister whom my mother came to pick who was just a year elder to me, since I enjoyed staying with my grandpa who prays, it didn’t bother me that mother left me with grandparents. I also enjoyed the evening when grandma called for the carriage and took me with her for an evening ride which also included visiting her daughters, sons and grandchildren.

I like to play with grandpa. One day, grandpa handed me a row egg and asked me to draw the egg. I knew what I was supposed to do but I took a piece of paper and drew the picture of an egg. When grandpa asked me between his work lifting up his head and looking towards me if I finished the egg I took the paper where I had the drawing of the egg and we laughed together, grandpa enjoyed my mischievous games, since I used to be sick as a child, I used to take up to 6 eggs a day which was not a difficult thing for me as a child.

When we were about 4 and 3 my sister and I were taken to the boarding school which was the practice in those days not because the school was far from the house. I was very happy to be among the nuns, loved and cared for, for I was about 3 very naughty but prayerful. In the chapel, sr. Anne Marie kept me close to her, and I would observe nuns at prayer. On Thursdays, Mother will come to visit us and take our laundry or send a driver to collect, then she will come to take us for the weekend. When she travels (which she loves to do) we stay with grandpa and grandma for the weekend.

Our family house had a beautiful garden, then we had a land which we called field. From the garden we had a gate to the field. I liked to go to play in the field and there was a big tortoise too and watch its movements. Sometimes, I follow the servant who goes to feed birds on the roofs.

One day our little puppy came running into the house. Mother did not like the dog in the house, so she gave a kick and it ran out crying and I didn’t like it so my pram (when I was a baby) was still in the corner, I took it and put the little puppy and made a few rounds till it was being consoled.

After the classes all the students went to the chapel, the children from the lower classes sat on the front. So I sat on the front bench watching intently at the Tabernacle while the priest opened it for adoration of the Blessed Sacrament and for the Benediction. Inside the Tabernacle there was a veil, below the veil I could see golden color, in my little mind, since I have heard from sisters that Jesus is in the Tabernacle, so I believed that it was baby Jesus sitting on a chair and his golden shoes were stricking out beneath, the veil. So day after day I sat in the chapel happy to see Jesus in the Tabernacle since I could see his shoes. When years passed, then I understood that it was the ciborium that I was looking at and I was not wrong, for, Jesus was in.

I was in the small children’s dormitory. All of us were in bed and we were not allowed to speak. I was naughty and playful. So I was talking to my friends in the next beds, our beds had dividing curtens. Usually, the sister in charge made a round to see if we were all in bed. As soon as we saw the sister was coming (she had a room divided with wooden panels and a door that opened to our dormitory) I started to pray Hail Mary and my friends answered from their beds Holy Mary till sister passed by then we giggled in bed over our mischief.

Our house was near a bay, across the bay is the church. I can hear the church bell as if from our garden, we can reach the church by road in a few minutes. Every week, Mother came to take us home. If she is not there, we are taken to our grandpa. One evening in the boarding, when all the small children were in bed, the moon was full and shining so brightly I went to call my friends from the bed, quietly went to the veranda outside the dormitory, in the moonlight, looking at the moon we were dancing and enjoying to our hearts’ content. Then we returned to our bed, believing that no one had seen our mischief.

Next day morning we were called to the sister - in - charges’ room. We reached it without knowing that our night adventure has come to light. Sr. gave us a good explanation why we should obey and we were not allowed to go home on the weekend as for me I was not sad about it because I had received 1st Holy Communion at the early age as the custom was and when I come back on Monday morning I had to make confession for not having attended the Holy Mass on Sunday.
